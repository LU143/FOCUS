Project Scope

Literature Survey

The literature review is essential to our research, providing a robust foundation for understanding and implementing a system to monitor student concentration levels in physical classrooms. We draw upon a diverse range of studies, each contributing valuable insights to different aspects of our research.

D'Mello et al.'s 2011 research on cognitive-affective states during complex learning, which explores the use of machine learning to map these states and assess student engagement and concentration, serves as a critical foundation for our work [1].

Yilmaz et al.'s 2019 study on real-time face detection and recognition, particularly for attendance tracking, aligns with our objectives for SubSystem 01, emphasizing the efficiency of MediaPipe-based face detection algorithms [2]. Our system takes this a step further by integrating MediaPipe with TensorFlow's Face Landmark Detection model to create a comprehensive real-time engagement assessment tool.

Litman et al.'s 2017 research demonstrates the potential of machine learning, particularly artificial neural networks (ANN), in predicting concentration levels, a crucial aspect of Sub-System 02 in our research [3]. Our system leverages multiple data streams, including face detection and sentiment analysis, to provide a richer understanding of student engagement.

Grafsgaard et al.'s 2012 research on analyzing facial expressions for affect and states in dyadic interaction is instrumental in developing our video segmentation and face recognition techniques [4].

Smith et al.'s 2018 research on automated question generation from educational content bridges the gap between post-processing materials and real-time analysis, ensuring that students remain engaged and actively participating throughout lectures [5].

Zhang et al.'s work in 2000 on multi-modal data fusion for semantic video indexing and retrieval provides a foundational understanding of creating comprehensive datasets from various data sources, allowing for a holistic view of student concentration levels [6].

Kahou et al.'s 2013 study on real-time Facial Action Unit Detection using Convolutional Neural Networks informs our video segmentation methods and helps refine our real-time face detection and recognition approach [7].

Miller's 2012 study on smart classrooms underscores the necessity and effectiveness of technology in enhancing the traditional classroom experience, informing our approach to deploying the system in physical classrooms [8].

These studies collectively contribute to the development of our comprehensive system for monitoring student concentration in physical classrooms, incorporating elements of real-time face recognition, machine learning, and multi-modal data analysis.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Identification & classification

Identification & classification of student engagement in the context of education and technology integration has seen significant advancements, but certain research gaps persist. One such gap is the absence of real-time assessment and intervention during live lectures. While prior work has focused on post-processing analytics, the need for systems that can instantly identify student concentration lapses and employ pedagogical strategies is evident. Real-time interventions hold promise in maintaining consistent engagement levels and improving learning outcomes. Additionally, the integration of multi-modal data sources for a holistic understanding of student engagement remains under-explored, despite its potential demonstrated in other domains.

Severity assessment

In the domain of student engagement, severity assessment is an essential facet. Current literature emphasizes the importance of cognitive-affective states, but limited attention is given to the interplay of external factors such as classroom environment, lecture delivery mode, or student-teacher ratios. Understanding how these variables influence cognitive-affective states could empower educators to adapt teaching methods and classroom settings accordingly. Moreover, the methodology for data collection lacks sophistication. While face detection and recognition are commonly used for attendance tracking, a more nuanced approach involving facial data, hand gestures, posture, and other behavioral cues could provide a more comprehensive insight into a student's engagement level.

Information sharing

Information sharing in the field of student engagement research is still incomplete, with a focus primarily on visual cues. Although visual cues like body language and facial expressions are important, auditory cues, such as the lecturer's tone, pitch, and modulation, have been relatively overlooked. Combining both visual and auditory cues could offer a more comprehensive understanding of classroom dynamics, enhancing the quality of information shared in this context. Addressing these research gaps will contribute to a more holistic approach to student engagement in education and technology integration.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

Enhancing Student Concentration in Physical Classrooms in the Digital Age

The research problem at hand revolves around addressing the declining concentration levels of students in physical classrooms in the digital age. The central question is how to identify and enhance student concentration in this setting. Various factors contribute to this issue, including the pervasive use of technology, distractions like smartphones and social media, and the need for instructional methods that engage digital-native students. Concentration is a complex phenomenon influenced by cognitive, emotional, and environmental factors, and it varies among diverse student populations. The challenge lies in collecting and interpreting data on concentration, integrating multi-modal information, and providing real-time interventions to regain student attention during live lectures. This aspect of live analysis and intervention is an underexplored area in current research.


Proposed Solution

The proposed solution aims to address the challenge of declining student concentration in physical classrooms during the digital age. This solution involves the development of an advanced intelligent system that harnesses state-of-the-art natural language processing techniques. The system's primary objectives are to enable real-time transcription of live lectures, filter out non-essential information, segment the content into sub-topics, and assess student concentration on each segment. Furthermore, it autonomously generates high-quality questions relevant to these sub-topics for continuous student assessment. To achieve this, the system first establishes real-time face detection within the classroom setting, allowing for the accurate recognition and analysis of student reactions. It then utilizes advanced machine learning algorithms to examine various cues, such as facial expressions and movements, to gauge students' concentration levels during lectures. Simultaneously, the system streamlines lecture content by transcribing it into coherent text and segmenting it into sub-topics using Natural Language Processing. By assessing students' concentration levels for each sub-topic and generating relevant questions, educators gain insights into student comprehension and engagement. The comprehensive scoring system, combining question performance metrics with observed concentration levels, provides a holistic view of each student's grasp of the content and overall engagement during the lecture. This intelligent framework promises to significantly enhance student participation, elevate learning outcomes, and improve the overall educational experience within physical classroom settings.

Research Objectives

01.Student Face Recognition and Attendance Tracking:

This is first objective of this research is the development of a cutting-edge video segmentation system for real-time detection and recognition of student faces. Utilizing technologies such as TensorFlow models and the faceapi.js library, this system not only identifies individual students but also matches them uniquely through labeled face descriptors. What sets this approach apart is its integration of attendance tracking, contributing to a holistic understanding of student engagement in the classroom. Through a refined methodology that emphasizes facial detection after initial image loading, significant improvements in both precision and speed of recognition procedures have been achieved.

02.Collect Multi-model Data and Analysis of Student Concentration Level:

This research objective centers on the acquisition of multi-modal data from students during lectures and the subsequent comprehensive analysis of their concentration levels. The system leverages TensorFlow.js and MediaPipe's face mesh technology to procure and process real-time data, which is then meticulously evaluated through a multi-layered neural network. This neural network, equipped with a softmax layer, ensures precise classification of concentration levels based on multi-modal datasets. Existing literature highlights the reliability of facial cues in indicating concentration levels, and this research extends this knowledge by establishing a real-time data-driven comprehension of student engagement, enabling instant feedback for educators. Figure 4 visually represents the training accuracy of the live concentration level model, showcasing the effectiveness of this approach.

03.Analyzing live lecture, Identifying Sub-topics andMonitoring Student Concentration Level or each Sub-topics:

The third research objective involves the analysis of live lectures to identify sub-paragraphs and sub-topics using NLP and transformer-based models. This system not only tracks the concentration level of students but also generates questions based on the lecture content. By employing tools such as Whisper, Scikit-learn, NLTK, Scipy, and TensorFlow, live lectures are transcribed, sub-topics are identified, and questions are generated, including multiple-choice questions for each sub-unit. Unlike previous research, which mainly focused on post-processing of lecture content, this work emphasizes real-time analysis, delivering immediate feedback and interventions to enhance student engagement and performance. This approach represents a significant advancement in educational technology, fostering more interactive and effective learning environments.

Methodology

Figure 2. High Level Architecture of the system.

The proposed pest and disease Surveillance system consists of 4 main components. They are;

  1. WCLWD and its symptom severity identification
  2. CCI Identification and progression level determination
  3. Deficiency Identification (Mg deficiency and Leaf Scorch Decline)
  4. Water resource identification
  5. Crowdsourcing for information sharing.

Fig 4 illustrates the overall system diagram of the proposed solution which was intended to provide a smart approach for stakeholders, researchers, and Coconut Development Officers (CDOs) to detect the coconut diseases and pest infestations that may affect the coconut industry. As shown in the diagram, the registered users of the system can capture or upload the images which are suspicious. The images are sent to the Amazon Web Services (AWS) backend server where the flask server is deployed. These images are processed in the flask server by the designed DCNN models for disease identification. If WCLWD is found, the symptom severity will be determined using CNN models. Simultaneously, if CCI is identified, the images will go through the Mask-R-CNN model to determine the progression level while the number of caterpillars are extracted using the YOLOv5 object detection algorithm. Images will be classified using the CNN models of Mg deficiency and LSD at the same time.

Once the system identifies that the leaves are infected, then the response will be captured by the crowdsourcing platform. The Google Map will be updated with the real time locations (latitude and longitude) of the infected palms. In Addition, the system will automatically send notifications to the farmers and other stakeholders who are at the risk of infection.
